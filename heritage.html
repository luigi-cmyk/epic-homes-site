Common Questions

Everything you're wondering about.

The honest answers to what most pre-construction buyers ask before they reserve.

What does "pre-construction" actually mean for me? +

It means construction hasn't started yet. The lot is secured, the base design is drawn, but the home is still on paper — which is exactly when changes are easiest and cheapest to make. You'll work with our design team to lock in finishes and any floor-plan adjustments before we break ground.

How much can I actually customize? +

A lot. Every finish is yours to choose: cabinets, counters, flooring, tile, paint, fixtures, lighting, exterior siding and trim. The floor plan is also adjustable — moving non-structural walls, swapping a bedroom for a home office, expanding the kitchen island, adjusting closet layouts. Major structural changes (footprint, roofline) are reviewed case by case.

How does the price compare to buying the home finished? +

A finished home in this neighborhood will appraise and list at the build cost plus the developer's equity buildup. Reserving pre-construction lets you lock in today's price, not tomorrow's market value. The exact savings depend on your finish choices, but reservation buyers consistently come in below comparable finished-home listings.

Is the reservation deposit refundable? +

Yes. The reservation deposit is fully refundable until you sign the construction contract after design. It exists to take the home off the public list while we walk you through finish selections — it's not a commitment to buy.

How does construction financing work for a pre-construction home? +

Most buyers use a construction-to-permanent loan from a local lender — we can introduce you to a few we've worked with for years. The bank funds the build in stages, then converts to a regular mortgage at move-in. We handle all the lender draw paperwork. If you'd rather pay cash or use a different structure, that works too.

What's the timeline from reservation to move-in? +

Plan on roughly 10–12 months from signed reservation to move-in: 4–6 weeks for design selections, 4–8 weeks for final permits, then 7–9 months of construction. We'll give you a realistic schedule before you commit, and we hit it.

What's included in the price — and what's not? +

The contract price includes everything: lot, design, permits, materials, labor, site work, your selected finishes, appliances, and final cleanup — delivered turnkey. The only things outside are your furniture and any custom landscaping beyond the basics. The price you sign is the price you pay.

Can I see the lot and meet the team before reserving? +

Absolutely — and you should. We'll meet you on Heritage Ct, walk the lot, talk through the design, and answer every question. There's no pressure to reserve at the visit, and there's no charge for the consultation.
